# FY2027 Membership Dues Restructure — Board Decision Brief

## Summary

The Alliance closed FY2025 with an operating deficit and has not restructured membership dues in
several years. Staff evaluated two dues scenarios against the 2026 member survey and peer
benchmarking. A flat increase was set aside after the survey indicated it would drive renewal
attrition high enough to offset the added revenue. Staff recommend a **tiered** dues increase for
FY2027.

## Financial context

The Alliance recorded an operating deficit of **$82,000** in FY2025. *[claim c001]* This deficit is
the gap the FY2027 dues decision is intended to close.

Benchmarking against similar-size regional alliances shows that peer associations charge, on average,
about **18% higher** standard dues than the Alliance currently does. *[claim c003]*

## Options considered

**Flat increase (not recommended).** A flat 15% dues increase was modeled as a way to close the
FY2027 budget gap. The 2026 member survey indicated that roughly **22% of surveyed members would not
renew** at a 15% increase — enough attrition that projected net revenue would fall short of the gap.
This option was therefore **set aside**. *[claim c002 — contradicted by survey evidence; superseded
by the tiered option below]*

**Tiered increase (recommended).** A tiered dues increase of **10% for standard members and 5% for
small-organization members** is projected to close the FY2027 gap with materially lower renewal
attrition than the flat increase. *[claim c004]*

## Recommendation

The board should adopt the tiered dues increase, effective FY2027. *[claim c005]*

Adoption is subject to the Alliance bylaws' amendment procedure, which requires a **two-thirds board
vote**. *[constraint]*

## Open items

- **Open question:** the projected member attrition at each candidate dues tier is not yet
  independently confirmed; the survey gives one estimate and staff recommend validating it before the
  vote. *[question q001]*
- **Assumption:** these projections assume FY2027 non-dues revenue holds flat at FY2025 levels.
  *[assumption]*
